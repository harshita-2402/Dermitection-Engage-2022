    About

    Dermitection is a deep learning based disease recognition application which covers 6 types of common skin diseases and 5 types of diseases related to eyes in humans.

    DIseases Covered

    SKIN DISEASES

    Acne and Rosacea

    A condition that causes redness and often small, red, pus-filled bumps on the face.

    Eczema

    A medical condition in which patches of skin become rough and inflamed with blisters which cause itching and bleeding.

    Light disease and disorders of pigmentation

    Thses disorders are caused by the loss of pigment-producing cells in the skin (melanocytes). It is thought to be an autoimmune disease. The white patches are very sensitive to the sun.

    Melanoma

    Melanoma is a form of skin cancer that begins in the cells (melanocytes) that control the pigment in your skin. It is the most serious form of skin cancer worldwide.

    Vascular Tumors

    A vascular tumor is a tumor of vascular origin; a soft tissue growth that can be either benign or malignant, formed from blood vessels or lymph vessels.

    Warts Molluscum and other Viral Infections

    A small, fleshy bump on the skin or mucous membrane caused by human papillomavirus. Warts are caused by various strains of human papillomaviruses.

    EYE DISEASES

    Bulging Eyes

    Bulging of the eyes causes the eyes to move out of the sockets in one or both eyes.

    Cataracts

    Clouding of the normally clear lens of the eye. Most cataracts develop slowly over the course of years. The main symptom is blurry vision.

    Crossed Eyes

    Disorder in which the eyes don't look in exactly the same direction at the same time. Causes of strabismus can include nerve injury or dysfunction of the muscles controlling the eye.

    Glaucoma

    A group of eye conditions that can cause blindness. With all types of glaucoma, the nerve connecting the eye to the brain is damaged, usually due to high eye pressure.

    Uveitis

    An inflammation of the middle layer of the eye (uvea). The most common type is an inflammation of the iris called iritis (anterior uveitis).
